About YIMBY Nederland

Over YIMBY Nederland

Georgist. Pro-abundance. Supply-side. We believe everyone deserves a home they can afford. Here's the thinking behind our approach.

Our Ideology

Onze Ideologie

We stand at the intersection of three powerful intellectual traditions, united by one practical goal: making housing abundant and affordable in the Netherlands.

Georgism: Smarter Taxation for Shared Prosperity

Henry George's foundational insight: land and natural resources are not created by human labour - their value comes from community, infrastructure, and location. When that value is privately captured, it concentrates wealth in the hands of landowners rather than rewarding productive work. When it's publicly captured through Land Value Tax, it funds public goods while eliminating the most economically efficient tax base: one with zero deadweight loss.

In the Dutch context, this means: before taxing a single euro of wages or business profit, exhaust every opportunity to tax land value, planning gains (planbatenheffing), congestion, pollution, and other contrived privileges. The hierarchy is clear - tax location before labour, tax externalities before enterprise.

YIMBY: Yes In My Back Yard

The YIMBY movement recognises that the housing crisis is fundamentally a supply crisis, driven by regulation that makes it illegal to build enough homes where people want to live. When local planning processes consistently block new housing, it's often renters, young people, and newcomers who pay the price.

YIMBY means saying yes to density, yes to apartments next to houses, yes to roof extensions and basement conversions, yes to building upward and inward rather than sprawling outward. It means recognising that every home not built is a family priced out.

Abundance: The Politics of More

The abundance approach asks a different question that dominates housing policy. Instead of rationing a fixed supply through ever-more-complex regulations, means-testing, and waiting lists, we ask: how do we produce so much housing that affordability is the natural outcome?

This isn't about ideology - it recognises the critical role of public housing (woningcorporaties), social safety nets, and public investment. But it insists that supply-side reform and public investment are complements, not substitutes. You cannot solve a shortage by redistributing scarcity more equitably; you solve it by ending the scarcity.

What This Means for Dutch Government

The Netherlands can lead Europe in housing innovation. The goal: make housing abundant, affordable, and accessible - the way food, clothing, and electronics have become abundant through markets combined with smart regulation.

This means reforming the barriers that make housing artificially scarce: parking minimums that inflate costs, welstandscommissies that substitute taste for rules, bezwaar procedures that let incumbents veto newcomers, height caps that create scarcity rents, and a tax system that punishes building while subsidising land ownership.

We can do better with: clear rules anyone can follow, permits approved by default, prices on externalities rather than bans on activity, land taxes rather than labour taxes, and continued investment in social housing funded by the value our communities create.

YIMBY: Ja In Mijn Achtertuin

De YIMBY-beweging erkent dat de woningcrisis fundamenteel een aanbodcrisis is, gedreven door regelgeving die het illegaal maakt om genoeg woningen te bouwen waar mensen willen wonen. Wanneer lokale planprocessen consequent nieuwe woningbouw blokkeren, zijn het vaak huurders, jongeren en nieuwkomers die de prijs betalen.

YIMBY betekent ja zeggen tegen dichtheid, ja tegen appartementen naast huizen, ja tegen dakopbouwen en kelderconversies, ja tegen omhoog en naar binnen bouwen in plaats van naar buiten uitspreiden. Het betekent erkennen dat elke woning die niet wordt gebouwd een gezin is dat wordt weggeprijsd.

Overvloed: De Politiek van Meer

De overvloedsbenadering stelt een andere vraag die het woningbeleid domineert. In plaats van een vast aanbod te rantsoeneren via steeds complexere regelgeving, toetsen en wachtlijsten, vragen we: hoe produceren we zoveel woningen dat betaalbaarheid het natuurlijke resultaat is?

Dit gaat niet over ideologie - het erkent de cruciale rol van publieke woningbouw (woningcorporaties), sociale vangnetten en publieke investeringen. Maar het houdt vol dat aanbodhervorming en publieke investering complementair zijn, geen substituten. About Hoog

Over Hoog

YIMBY Nederland is an initiative of the Hoog YouTube channel. We create video content exploring Dutch housing policy, urban planning, and the case for abundance - combining investigative journalism with Georgist economic analysis.

When you report a housing delay, you're not just venting - you're providing evidence. We investigate the most compelling cases and turn them into content that reaches tens of thousands, building public pressure for the reforms described on our policy platform.

YIMBY Nederland is een initiatief van het Hoog YouTube-kanaal. Wij maken videocontent die Nederlands woningbeleid, stedenbouw en de zaak voor overvloed verkent - met een combinatie van onderzoeksjournalistiek en Georgistische economische analyse.

Wanneer je een bouwvertraging meldt, lucht je niet alleen je hart - je levert bewijs. We onderzoeken de meest overtuigende gevallen en maken er content van die tienduizenden bereikt, waarmee we publieke druk opbouwen voor de hervormingen beschreven op ons beleidsplatform.
